What is a Collocation?

A collocation is a pair or group of words that habitually co-occur in natural language — not because grammar demands it, but because usage patterns have made them conventional. Native speakers acquire collocations implicitly; L2 learners and AI models often produce near-misses that are grammatically correct but lexically unnatural.

Example Pairs

✓ Corpus-attested She made a decision to leave early.
✗ L2 / AI pattern She took a decision to leave early.
✓ Corpus-attested The research raises awareness of the issue.
✗ L2 / AI pattern The research increases awareness of the issue.

Why AI Language Models Struggle

Large language models are trained on enormous amounts of text, yet they can still produce collocational errors. This happens because LLMs optimise for statistical plausibility across a distribution of text rather than acquiring the deep lexical knowledge that underlies native-speaker intuition. The result: fluent-sounding sentences with slightly off-target word choices.

Common AI patterns include: synonym substitution (conduct research → perform research), calques from L1 (e.g. French/Spanish learners writing do a photo for take a photo), and over-generalisation of productive patterns.

Corpus Linguistics & Collocations

Corpus linguistics analyses large, real-world text collections to reveal how words actually combine. Tools like Sketch Engine, the British National Corpus, and COCA (Corpus of Contemporary American English) allow researchers to measure collocation strength using statistics such as MI score (Mutual Information) and t-score.

For SLA researchers, corpus data provides an empirical baseline against which learner output — and now AI output — can be compared.

Implications for ELT & MA English Studies

As AI writing tools become widespread, learners may begin to internalise AI-generated patterns as models of correct English. This raises important questions for language pedagogy: How do we teach collocation awareness in an AI-saturated environment? How do we help learners develop the critical linguistic literacy to evaluate AI output?

Awareness-raising activities — like this quiz — help develop the metalinguistic sensitivity needed to notice collocational norms and deviations.
