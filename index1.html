Collocations are words that often go together in English. For example, we say “make a decision” (not “do a decision”) and “pay attention” (not “make attention”). Learning these common combinations will make your English sound more natural.

This quiz helps you practise recognising which word pairs are used by native speakers and which ones sound odd – even if they are grammatically correct.

Use these free tools to check real‑world usage and explore collocations in context:

  • YouGlish – YouTube subtitles
  • COCA – 1‑billion‑word corpus
  • Fraze.It – phrases in context
  • FreeCollocation – collocation dictionary
  • WordReference Collocations – academic writing
